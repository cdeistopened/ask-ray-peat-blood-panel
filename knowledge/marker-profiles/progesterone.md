# Progesterone

**Reliability:** Tier 2 -- Peat found serum progesterone useful but with the caveat that brain concentrations are ~10x blood levels, so serum underrepresents the most important site of action. Saliva tests are unreliable because progesterone is nearly insoluble in water.
**Companion markers:** Estradiol (the E2:P4 ratio is the real marker), cortisol, thyroid, temperature, pulse
**Peat's range:** Women endogenous luteal: ~30 mg/day production (serum 5-20 ng/mL). Therapeutic: 10-30 mg general; 100 mg for acute migraine. Men: 5-10 mg safe. Pregnancy: 800-900 mg/day (demonstrates safety margin).
**Mainstream range:** Follicular: <1.5 ng/mL; Luteal: 5-20 ng/mL; Pregnancy third trimester: 65-290 ng/mL; Male: 0.3-1.2 ng/mL

## Peat's Position

Progesterone was the centerpiece of Peat's therapeutic framework -- the most protective hormone in the body. The brain has the highest concentration of progesterone of any tissue, approximately ten times higher than blood levels, making it an essential brain-stabilizing molecule. A healthy late pregnancy produces 800-900 mg of progesterone daily from the placenta, demonstrating an enormous safety margin. Katharina Dalton's treated patients (working-class mothers, average IQ 90) produced babies averaging 130 IQ, with intelligence correlating dose-dependently with the amount of progesterone given.

Peat's central claim about progesterone is that it opposes estrogen through roughly ten simultaneous mechanisms: it destroys the estrogen receptor when it binds to the progesterone receptor, turns off estrogen production, changes estrogen metabolism toward detoxification, and activates clearance enzymes. This is not a gentle modulation but an absolute anti-estrogenic effect. If continuous estrogen exposure is interrupted by periodic progesterone, cancer does not develop. Lipschutz called pregnenolone and progesterone the "anti-fibromatogenic steroids" -- substances that inhibit fibrous overproduction and protect against tumors.

Progesterone provides stress protection equivalent to cortisol but without the catabolic tissue destruction. Pregnant rats with adrenals removed survived stresses that killed non-pregnant adrenalectomized rats -- progesterone alone provided complete stress protection without any cortisol. This is possible because progesterone supports oxidative metabolism directly, stabilizes membranes, and blocks the inflammatory cascade at multiple points. It has antihistamine effects, anti-serotonin effects, steadies heart rhythm the way digitalis does, and blocks aldosterone-driven cardiac fibrosis. It even protects against radiation injury because radiation's cellular effects parallel estrogen's.

The critical distinction in the corpus is between natural progesterone and synthetic progestins (medroxyprogesterone). Synthetic progestins block conversion to the natural range of downstream steroids and interfere with brain function. The naming causes dangerous confusion -- progestins are anti-progesterone, not progesterone.

## Mainstream Inversion

Mainstream medicine treats estrogen as the primary female hormone and progesterone as secondary -- mainly relevant to pregnancy and menstrual regulation. Peat inverted this completely: progesterone is the primary protective hormone for both sexes, while estrogen is a stress hormone. Mainstream prescribes synthetic progestins interchangeably with progesterone; Peat considered progestins to be anti-progesterone agents that interfere with brain function and block the natural steroid cascade.

Mainstream endocrinology also treats menopausal symptoms as estrogen deficiency. Peat argued the real deficiency is progesterone: in rat studies, ovariectomy restores normal estrogen levels within a week (from adrenal/peripheral sources), but progesterone disappears. The "estrogen deficiency" model led to HRT with estrogen, which Peat considered adding fuel to the fire.

In the 1930s, progesterone deficiency was recognized as a diagnostic sign of hypothyroidism. This connection has been largely forgotten by modern medicine.

## Sex Differences

For women, progesterone is the critical hormone to assess. A woman produces about 30 mg/day during the luteal phase. Fertility requires 50-100 times as much progesterone as estrogen. Women who have been pregnant eight times have higher residual progesterone levels and live much longer. If progesterone supplements are used continuously all month, the liver upregulates excretion over two weeks; the natural cycle's two-week rest allows enzyme reset. Peat recommended cycling progesterone two weeks on, two weeks off for premenopausal women.

For men, 5-10 mg of progesterone is in the normal range and reduces estrogen production, making testosterone's effect more potent. Higher doses (30 mg) can antagonize testosterone in men, so the dose must stay low. Because testosterone can be converted to estrogen via aromatase, progesterone is actually more protective than testosterone in aging -- it cannot be aromatized.

In both sexes, progesterone's brain concentration is ~10x blood levels, making serum measurements a significant underestimate of neurological progesterone status.

## Medication Effects

- **Natural progesterone (Prometrium, compounded):** 10-30 mg for general effects; 100 mg for acute migraine (complete reversal in 1-2 minutes). Women cycle 2 weeks on, 2 weeks off. Men use 5-10 mg. Enormous safety margin (800-900 mg/day in pregnancy).
- **Synthetic progestins (medroxyprogesterone/Provera):** Not progesterone. Block natural steroid conversion, interfere with brain function. Avoid entirely.
- **Birth control pills:** Estrogen component raises prolactin and cortisol; progestin component is anti-progesterone. Net effect is estrogen dominance.
- **Thyroid supplementation:** Required for progesterone synthesis. In the 1930s, progesterone deficiency was a diagnostic sign of hypothyroidism.
- **Pregnenolone:** Upstream precursor; converts to progesterone according to the body's need. The safest first-line intervention.
- **Vitamin A:** Required for cholesterol-to-pregnenolone-to-progesterone conversion. Deficiency causes progesterone deficiency.
- **Statins/low-cholesterol diets:** Deplete the raw material for all steroid synthesis including progesterone.
