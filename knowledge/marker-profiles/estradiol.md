# Estradiol / Estrogen

**Reliability:** Tier 4 -- Peat explicitly distrusted serum estrogen. Tissue estrogen increases with age while serum drops, making blood tests actively misleading. Each lab has its own standards, and reference populations are typically sick.
**Companion markers:** Progesterone (essential -- the E2:P4 ratio is the real marker), SHBG, aromatase activity, body temperature, thyroid
**Peat's range:** No specific serum target. Fertility requires P4:E2 ratio of 50:1 to 100:1. Functional assessment via temperature is preferred.
**Mainstream range:** Premenopausal E2: varies by cycle phase (follicular 30-120 pg/mL, mid-cycle 100-400 pg/mL, luteal 50-250 pg/mL); Postmenopausal: <20 pg/mL; Male: 10-40 pg/mL

## Peat's Position

Peat's most radical departure from mainstream endocrinology was his classification of estrogen as a stress hormone, not a sex hormone. His 1972 dissertation established that estrogen's effects on cells are indistinguishable from the effects of X-rays, aging, and oxygen deprivation -- all damage cytochrome oxidase, shifting cells from oxidative metabolism toward glycolysis. Estrogen damages mitochondria in at least six to eight different ways: it impairs glucose oxidation, poisons the enzyme that uses oxygen, and blocks cholesterol's conversion to protective steroids. This creates a vicious cycle where estrogen simultaneously poisons energy production and prevents the synthesis of hormones that oppose it.

Serum estrogen is one of the most misleading lab values in Peat's framework. With aging, serum estrogen declines, but tissue estrogen actually increases. Without progesterone to mobilize it, estrogen binds to receptors and accumulates intracellularly. Doctors see low serum estrogen in a postmenopausal woman and prescribe estrogen replacement, when the biological reality is estrogen dominance at the tissue level. After menopause, estrogen forms inside cells throughout the body -- fat cells, skin, breast, and uterus -- from adrenal precursors via the enzyme aromatase. Any injury to any tissue activates aromatase, making estrogen production a marker and perpetuator of defensive metabolism.

The three main estrogens -- estradiol (most potent), estrone, and estriol (weakest) -- exist in a hierarchy of harm. A healthy metabolism converts potent estradiol to weaker estriol rapidly. During pregnancy, a high estriol index indicates health because estradiol is being destroyed quickly. The liver is the primary site of estrogen detoxification: a well-nourished liver prepares 100% of estrogen for excretion. Malnutrition, hypothyroidism, and fasting impair this detoxification, allowing estrogen to recirculate.

Peat viewed estrogen as fundamentally excitotoxic: it increases glutamate, decreases GABA, and excites brain cells while impairing their use of oxygen and glucose. A shot of estrogen can cause immediate psychosis. Contraceptive estrogen was linked to strokes in young women. Continuous uninterrupted estrogen exposure follows a carcinogenic sequence: uterus, then breast, then lungs, kidneys, intestine, liver, and brain. Progesterone interrupts this sequence by destroying the estrogen receptor, activating estrogen-eliminating enzymes, and opposing estrogen through roughly ten different mechanisms simultaneously.

## Mainstream Inversion

Mainstream medicine treats estrogen as the essential female hormone whose decline at menopause causes hot flashes, bone loss, and cognitive decline. Peat argued these symptoms are better explained by falling progesterone and thyroid, not estrogen deficiency. In rat studies, removing ovaries restored normal estrogen levels within a week (from adrenal/peripheral production) -- the real loss was progesterone. Mainstream promotes estrogen replacement therapy; Peat considered this adding fuel to a fire.

Mainstream also celebrates estrogen's role in bone health based on rat studies. Peat noted that estrogen strengthens bones in rats (nocturnal animals) but weakens bones in dogs and humans (diurnal animals). The estrogen industry cherry-picked the rat model.

The mainstream view that estrogen is the feminizing hormone is also inverted: Peat cited evidence that estrogen is the masculinizing hormone during fetal development, with female fetuses protected by alpha-fetoprotein. Progesterone, not estrogen, is the feminizing hormone.

## Sex Differences

In premenopausal women, the estrogen-to-progesterone ratio is the critical number, not absolute estrogen. Almost everyone around age 40 reaches their lifetime peak E:P ratio -- the most dangerous hormonal configuration. Fertility requires 50-100 times as much progesterone as estrogen. Women have 5-10 times as much thyroid disease as men because estrogen blocks hepatic T4-to-T3 conversion. If progesterone supplements are used continuously all month, the liver upregulates excretion over two weeks, rendering them ineffective; cycling two weeks on, two weeks off matches the natural menstrual rhythm and allows liver enzyme reset.

In men, estrogen rises sharply under stress, poor diet, accidents, and in old age with degenerative diseases. Aromatase-knockout mice (no estrogen production) have twice as much testosterone in the brain and age without dementia. Yeast in alcoholic beverages produces actual human estradiol, making beer and wine direct estrogenic exposures.

Postmenopausal women are particularly vulnerable because progesterone production fails while estrogen continues forming in peripheral tissues. Low serum estrogen with low progesterone equals estrogen dominance, not estrogen deficiency.

## Medication Effects

- **Birth control pills:** Chronic estrogen elevation causes epidemic of prolactin-secreting pituitary tumors, stroke risk, and progressive cancerization. Suppresses TSH and disrupts the entire hormonal cascade.
- **Estrogen replacement therapy (HRT):** Peat considered this harmful. Symptoms attributed to estrogen deficiency at menopause are caused by low thyroid and progesterone.
- **Progesterone (natural, not synthetic progestins):** Anti-estrogen in at least ten ways. Destroys estrogen receptor, activates clearance enzymes, interrupts carcinogenic sequence.
- **Thyroid supplementation:** Restores hepatic T4-to-T3 conversion that estrogen impairs. Supports liver estrogen detoxification.
- **Raw carrot/bamboo shoots/mushrooms:** Fiber binds estrogen in bile, preventing enterohepatic recirculation.
- **Aspirin:** Anti-estrogen effects alongside prostaglandin blockade.
- **Aromatase inhibitors (pharmaceutical or dietary):** Block conversion of testosterone/DHEA to estrogen. Oranges (naringenin) and guavas (apigenin) are natural sources.
- **SSRIs:** Raise serotonin, which promotes estrogen production and inflammation; worsen estrogen dominance.
- **Statins/low-fat diets:** Lower cholesterol, preventing formation of protective steroids that oppose estrogen.
